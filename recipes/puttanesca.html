Spaghetti alla Puttanesca

Description

Spaghetti alla puttanesca is an Italian pasta dish invented in Naples in the mid-20th century and made typically with tomatoes, olive oil, olives, anchovies, chili peppers, capers, and garlic—with vermicelli or spaghetti pasta.

The name refers either to the possibility that the dish was invented in a brothel or to the fact that Italians use puttana and related words as a general-use profanity, so spaghetti alla puttanesca could roughly mean spaghetti made with some random shit.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces pasta
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chopped tomatoes, pushed through a sieve
  • 4 anchovy filets, rinsed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 tablespoons capers
  • 20 Greek olives, pitted and coarsely chopped
  • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
  2. Cook pasta in boiling water for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet over low heat; cook garlic in oil until golden.
  4. Add sieved tomatoes, and cook 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in anchovies, tomato paste, capers, olives, and red pepper flakes.
  6. Cook 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Toss pasta with sauce, and serve.
